# Kidtinerary Phase II — Friends & Family Validation Survey

Date: 2026-04-24
Purpose: Pressure-test the Phase II thesis (rolodex + planner + nudges, NOT
discovery) with real parents before building it.

Linked doc: [2026-04-24-phase-ii-rolodex-overhaul.md](./2026-04-24-phase-ii-rolodex-overhaul.md)

---

## How to administer this survey

**Sample:** 10–20 parents you know who have kids between ages 3 and 14. Mix of
situations: only-child parents, multi-kid parents, working parents, SAHPs, new
transplants, long-tenured locals. Avoid sampling only your most organized
friends — bias toward variety of organization styles.

**Format options:**
- Google Form (recommended — copy questions below; supports branching for
  rating scales)
- Typeform (better completion rate, nicer aesthetic)
- Raw text via SMS/email to 5–8 closest friends (highest quality answers,
  smallest sample)

**Estimated completion time:** 8–10 minutes. Tell respondents this up front.

**Incentive:** Optional but helpful. A $10 gift card to the first 10
respondents usually doubles completion. If that feels weird with friends, a
promise of "first access when it ships" is fine.

**Survey structure:**
- **Section 1 (Q1–Q2):** Context
- **Section 2 (Q3–Q8):** Current behavior — **no solution described yet,
  keep unbiased**
- **Section 3 (Q9–Q11):** Problem validation — rating scales
- **Section 4 (Q12–Q16):** Solution reaction — Kidtinerary described here for
  the first time
- **Section 5 (Q17–Q18):** Sharing behavior
- **Section 6 (Q19–Q20):** Open-ended

### Section 4 — Reaction to Kidtinerary

*(Read this short description before answering the rest of this section.)*

> **Kidtinerary** is a personal tool for parents to catalog every activity
> their kid is doing, considering, or has ever done — a lifetime rolodex of
> camps, classes, lessons, sports, and more. You add things by pasting a
> link, forwarding an email, or typing it in; Kidtinerary fills in the
> details automatically. It plans your kid's week, reminds you when
> registration opens or deadlines approach, and remembers everything from
> past years. It's private by default, but you can send any activity or
> list to another parent via text with one tap.

> It is **not** a search engine or catalog of camps — we think Google and
> your group chats already do that well.

## How to read the results

*(Internal — don't share this with respondents.)*

### Thesis-validating signals

- **Q9 < Q10 and Q9 < Q11** — validates the "organization > discovery"
  thesis. If Q9 averages highest, the thesis is at risk.
- **Q7 answers skew to "2–3" or more** — validates the reminders/nudges
  pillar. If most say "never," reminders matter less than assumed.
- **Q8 answers skew toward "Often" or "Constantly"** — validates the
  year-over-year memory bet.
- **Q13 top 3 includes "Saving anything in one place" + "Planner" +
  "Reminders"** — validates Phase II P0 prioritization.
- **Q17 skews toward "text a link to Kidtinerary"** — validates outbound
  share-sheet as sufficient (no in-app social needed).

### Thesis-invalidating signals — be honest

- **Q9 > Q10** — parents really do want discovery help. Reconsider the
  "no catalog" stance. Ask follow-ups.
- **Q12 low with Q16 mentioning "I already use [X]"** — someone else already
  owns this space in their mental model. Find out who.
- **Q14 is mostly "I wouldn't want reminders from yet another app"** — push
  notifications / SMS are not the growth lever assumed. Email-only or
  integration with existing calendars may matter more.
- **Q18 skews to "I wouldn't look at it either way"** — sharing isn't a
  growth mechanism at all, even passive. Cut the share-sheet from P0
  and focus exclusively on the personal-tool use case.
- **Q13 top choices don't include any of the Phase II P0 items** — the plan
  is wrong. Do more interviews before shipping.

### Questions to ask in a follow-up conversation

Pick 3–5 respondents whose answers were most extreme (most enthusiastic OR
most skeptical) and ask:
- Walk me through the last time you signed a kid up for something. Where
  did the information live between finding it and signing up?
- If you could only have one feature from the list in Q13, which would it
  be and why?
- (If they already use another tool) What do you love about it? What's
  missing?
- (If they missed a registration in Q7) Walk me through how it happened.

### Sample size reality check

- **< 10 responses:** directional only. Useful for gut-check, not decisions.
- **10–25 responses:** directional + some pattern-finding. Good for a
  personal-network validation round.
- **25+ responses:** starts to be patterns. Still not statistically
  significant, but robust enough to kill or keep major directions.

### What to do if the thesis holds

Proceed with Phase II as written. Use Q13 rankings to re-prioritize P0 if
anything is clearly above/below where the plan put it.

### What to do if the thesis doesn't hold

Don't pivot immediately. Run 5 live 30-min interviews with a mix of
enthusiastic and skeptical respondents to understand *why* before changing
the plan. Survey signals are cheap; interviews are what actually change
strategy.
