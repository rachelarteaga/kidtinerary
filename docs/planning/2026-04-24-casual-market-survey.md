## Google Form — 10 questions

## Notes for Rachel

**IG story tips**
- post all 7 slides in one story drop so people can swipe through
- the multiple choice sticker caps at 4 options — already sized for that
- question stickers surface responses for you to screenshot later; free
  text answers on slide 7 are where the gold lives
- 7 slides is right at the attention-budget edge; drop-off on slide 7
  is real but the people who do answer are your highest-signal
  respondents

**Google Form tips**
- set Q9 + Q10 to long-answer (paragraph) so responses aren't truncated
- don't add a "what's your name" field — anonymous gets you more honest
  vents
- share via: text to 5–10 closest parent friends first, then post the
  link in your story or on stories with "help me out" framing
- if you want to track responses over time, turn on "collect email
  addresses" (optional, Google Form setting) — but this kills response
  rate. skip it unless you really need to follow up.

**Reading the Google Form results (later)**
- if Q4 skews to "keeping track" or "both" → validates the organizing
  thesis
- if Q4 skews to "finding" → worth a follow-up convo, but don't panic;
  one skew doesn't flip a plan
- Q9 + Q10 are where the actual product insights live. read every one.
  patterns across vents > any single poll stat.

**Reading the IG results (later) — what each slide tells you**
- **Slide 1 (finding vs tracking):** same as Google Q4 — the headline
  thesis test. "Keeping track" winning = thesis holds.
- **Slide 4 (how do you remember signups):** if "i don't remember lol"
  wins → the nudges pillar has a huge wedge. if "cal reminder" wins →
  you're replacing a calendar event, so nudges have to be meaningfully
  better than that. if "write myself a note" wins → fragile system,
  still a wedge.
- **Slide 5 (where does info live):** tells you which tool you're
  actually competing with. "my brain" or "group chat" winning = no
  incumbent, green light. "notes app" or "google cal" winning = people
  have working systems, Kidtinerary needs a real differentiator to beat
  them.
- **Slide 6 (friend share):** validates the outbound-share-sheet bet.
  "text with a link" winning = sharing via text is the natural primary
  action, which is exactly what's planned.
- **Slide 7 (vent + wishlist):** same as Google Q9 + Q10 — read every
  one, pattern-match the themes. most people will just vent; the few
  who also answer the "what would help" half are your highest-signal
  respondents.
